Optional, Do [Step 7](#7-story) at the same time as this, as thematically it may make sense.

This is mainly human versus singer. Singers get starting forms that provide different benefits, while Humans gain additional Expertise at different levels.
### 1.2. Choose Culture (pg.38)
This is where you are from (Azish / Alethi / Iri / etc) you get two cultural expertise, these give bonuses and background on those specific cultures along with allowing you to speak that language

## 3. Attributes (pg.18)
Attributes start at 0 and max out at 5^, but when making a character you can only put up to 3 in a single attribute. Cosmere RPG gives a pool of 12 points to apply to your attributes in whichever way you wish. Each attribute will add to the associated skills on your Character sheet, so if you want to use big hammers then you want to have a good `Heavy Weaponry (Str)` skill, make sure you have lots of strength! Or if you want to pursuade others, `Pursuasion (Pre)` and Presence is your your go to!
 
Character Statistics are discussed further in (c3 pg. 47) and Skills are next!

^5 is is the max, but certain abilities, notably singer forms, can push this higher.
```
Physical Defense = Strength + Speed  

Cognitive Defense = Intellect + Willpower  

Spiritual Defense = Awareness + Presence
```

## 4. Skills and Expertises

### 4.1 Skills pg.47
What are Skill Ranks? These are the bubbles next to each skill on your character sheet. They notate how "good" you are at a particular thing.

You get 1 free skill rank tied to your [Heroic Path](#2-choose-starting-heroic-path-pg69) which you can add first. Then add 4 more ranks wherever seems the most appropriate, but with the restriction that you may not add more than 2 to any skill within character creation. 

You may now notate your skill modifiers on your character sheet.
```
Skill Modifier = Attribute Value + Number of Skill Rank + Any Other Modifiers
```
When rolling for a particular skill, described as a D20 Test, you add this modifier
```
D20 Test Result = Skill Modifier + D20
```

### 4.2 Expertises pg.52
Expertises are vague on purpose, these are meant to leave room for your character to be who they are. Having Expertises in a particular thing means that you will likely not have to role in some circumstances to use said thing or complete an objective. You are an Expert, you just do the thing. 

This also means that situations that you are able to roll for completing certain objectives that would be impossible for others. Your character will automatically get 2 Expertises based on their culture, but you also get more based on your intelligence score (1 Expertise per point in Int)

Reviewing the book on page 52 is best for this.
## 5. Talents
What are Talents? These are "skills" or "abilities" in the various paths / subpaths that effect what your character can do. They tend to give passive or active bonuses or different ways of accomplishing tasks. When given the ability to select a new Talent, unless otherwise specified, you can select a Talent from any path tree in which you meet the requirements and you own a talent that is connected to it from a line. The only exception to this is the starting Talents to the various Heroic paths.

In [Step Two](#2-choose-starting-heroic-path-pg69) you selected your Heroic Path, this includes a starting talent that you can document on your character sheet.

Next, depending on your [Ancestry](#11-choose-ancestry-pg29), you will gain another Talent
- Singers gain "Change Forms"
- Humans gain another talent available to them, this does mean as a human you have start on two Heroic Paths at level 1

## 7. Story

Finally the good stuff right? we are through most of the data and note taking, we know who you are as a character, but what do you want? What is your purpose? What kind of story do you want to tell?

Remember, you are a ***Hero***, regardless of your background, Cosmere RPG is about being a hero, so these next several steps should have a hint of main character syndrome.

### 7.1. Purpose and Obstacles
Both of these could be a short list of items, but try to single it down to a core idea or thought.

Choose your **Purpose**: This is what drives you, this is why you are here, these could be lofty goals in your life. It could be a lot of things, you could want to:
- Protect those that are weak
- Heal from past trauma
- Bring peace to your homeland
- Free those who have been enslaved.

This is obviously non-exhaustive, but your purpose can be internal or external, or a combination of both, it is up for you to decide.

Choose your **Obstacle**: This is what is holding you back, and is often something that stands in direct opposition to your Purpose. You may be held back by
- Trying to live up to the standards of your parents
- Failing to save those close to you
- Never trusting your own direction

### 7.2. Choose Your Goals
These are tangible things that your character wants. There is not a requirement as to how many you have but they should all culminate to your Purpose. Some of them may be immediate things that your character can do, while some may be longer term goals that they are working towards.

They also come with rewards, completing a goal will reward you with valuable connections, possessions (fabrials, shardplate, etc), titles (becoming a noble), and more

**Importantly** you can add new goals in the future, and your GM may ask you to do so depending on where in the story you have gotten. There is a location on the character sheet for these and to notate your status in them. You need to make at least one goal now.

### 7.3. Additional Background pg.23

Now that you have a reasonable idea of what your character is, and who they are. Consider some final thoughts on your character. The book contains more details, but consider these facets of your character. Occupation, Existing Relationships, Loyalties, Personality, Appearance, Possible Radiant Order.

you don't need these to be locked down eventually and can let them evolve over time.

## 8. Final Calculations

There is some final cleanup if you haven't yet

```
Health = 10 + Strength + other bonuses

Focus = 2 + Willpower + other bonuses
```

Investiture will be 0 for now, until, and if, you join a Radiant Path, after doing so you will gain a max amount of investiture (pg.54) that mechanically works similar to focus.

```
Physical Defense = 10 + Strength + Speed + other bonuses

Cognitive Defense = 10 + Intellect + Willpower + other bonuses

Spiritual Defense = 10 + Awareness + Presence + other bonuses
```

You are finished with your character and you are ready to play the Game!
